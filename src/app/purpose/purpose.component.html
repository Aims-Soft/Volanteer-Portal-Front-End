Our Purpose

Promoting fair resource distribution and mutual cooperation among mosques involves leveraging their role as community hubs to foster economic justice, social cohesion, and shared prosperity. By adopting models that emphasize transparency, collaboration, and structured management, mosques can move beyond traditional worship to actively empower their congregations and surrounding communities, aligning with Islamic principles of ta'awun (mutual aid) and social solidarity. These initiatives transform mosques into central, sustainable hubs that provide not only spiritual guidance but also economic and social services, strengthening the community's overall resilience.

Why Mother Mosques?

Mother mosques serve as central hubs that manage resource distribution and oversee the network

Why Daughter Mosques?

Smaller mosques connected to receive resources and support from mother mosque
